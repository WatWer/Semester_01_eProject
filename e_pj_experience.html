DRAGON PARK

Dragon Park offers a magical world with rides inspired by well-known fairytales. Children can also join mini tours to explore Paris and the Eiffel Tower through the Tour de Paris. The highlight of the theme park is the Phi Long Than Toc rollercoaster – the longest in Asia at a length of 1.1 kilometres . There are many bus throught througt dinosaurs park . All rides have been imported from Europe. With more than 100,000 flowers and plants and 10,000 trees, Dragon Park will also attract tourists due to its scenery and diverse high quality restaurants.

WATER PARK

A water park or waterpark is an amusement park that features water play areas such as swimming pools, water slides, splash pads, water playgrounds, and lazy rivers, as well as areas for floating, bathing, swimming, and other barefoot environments. Modern water parks may also be equipped with some type of artificial surfing or bodyboarding environment, such as a wave pool or flowrider.

SKY DROP

As a highest heart-pumping vertical free fall, Sky Drop occupies a super high place between 455 and 485 meters. It holds the Guinness World Records® title - The Highest Trill Ride. It offers two styles, a traditional “Sitting Drop” and a free-falling “Standing Drop”, whereas the city can feel your heart beat.

ALPINE COASTER

The longest alpine roller coaster in the world is without doubt the highlight of the outdoor holiday region of Hoch-Imst. Whether you spend the day there with the popular book of ten tickets, or combine this fun-filled activity with other sporty activities such as hiking or climbing, for many children, the Alpine Coaster is often the star attraction of a family trip to Hoch-Imst.

BUMPER CAR

Bumper cars or dodgems is the generic name for a type of flat ride consisting of several small electrically powered cars which draw power from the floor and/or ceiling, and which are turned on and off remotely by an operator. They are also known as bumping cars, dodging cars and dashing cars.
